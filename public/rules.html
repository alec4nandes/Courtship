Courtship Card Game Rules

play game

  • Each player starts off with 60 HP and 5 cards
  • Each hand plays 1-4 numbered cards with an optional court card
    • Each hand must play at least one numbered card
      • Players with only court cards cannot play a hand. They must pull another card
    • A court card calls for more numbered cards in a hand. The rank of the court card determines the extra cards
      • No court cards: 1 numbered card only
      • Jack: 2 numbered cards
      • Queen: 3 numbered cards
      • King: 4 numbered cards
    • Players can only play one court card per turn
    • Players must have at least 5 cards at the start of each turn. If a player has less than 5 cards after playing their hand, they must draw cards until they have 5, unless the draw pile is exhausted
    • For each turn, players can either draw a card or play a hand
  • Turns are for either recovery points, where the numbered cards are all Hearts, or attack points, where the numbered cards are not Hearts. A court card can always be any suit
    • Recovery points return HP to the player
    • Attack points reduce the HP of the opponent
    • A turn can only be for one kind of point. No mixing Hearts with other suits in the numbered cards.
  • Point calculation
    • Double a card's points if it is the same suit as a court card just played by the opponent
    • Double the total attack or recovery points if the player's court card is the same suit as a court card just played by the opponent. Example:
      • Opponent plays Jack of Clubs, 8 of Spades, and 5 of Clubs
      • You take 8 + 5 = 13 points damage
      • Then you play Queen of Clubs, 7 of Diamonds, 4 of Clubs, and 2 of Spades
      • Opponent takes 2(7 + 4*2 + 2) = 34 points damage
  • Play until someone runs out of HP, or the draw pile and hands are exhausted (highest HP wins)
